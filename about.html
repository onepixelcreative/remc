About the founder

Liam Macleod, Director of REMC.

A decade pricing UK electrical tenders. Based in Glasgow, working with contractors across the United Kingdom — from domestic refurbishments in the Highlands to industrial schemes in the South East.

I'm Liam — the founder and director of REMC Ltd. I started this business in 2024 after a decade of pricing electrical tenders in-house for UK contracting firms, because I kept watching the same problem play out: small and mid-size electrical contractors needing the discipline of a senior estimator, but unable to justify the £75k+ loaded cost of hiring one full-time.

The background

I spent my career before REMC inside electrical contracting businesses, working up from estimator to senior estimator and then commercial lead across a range of project values — from £30k retail fit-outs to £780k industrial schemes. Most of it commercial and industrial, with periods on healthcare and education frameworks.

The pattern that pushed me to start REMC: in nearly every business I worked in, the directors were doing the pricing themselves. Not because they wanted to, but because they couldn't find someone competent at a price the business could afford. They priced tenders at night and on weekends, missed bids they should have won, and watched margin leak out of jobs that were under-qualified at submission.

Outsourcing the estimating function was the obvious fix — but the existing options in the UK market were either freelance estimators with no methodology, or large consultancies priced for tier-one work. There was nothing in the middle that felt like a proper in-house function delivered remotely. So I built it.

Based in Glasgow, working UK-wide

REMC operates from Glasgow, Scotland. Everything we do is digital — Bluebeam take-offs, Excel BOQs, branded submission packs — which means geography doesn't matter to the service. We work with electrical contractors from Inverness to Cornwall, with no premium for being outside Scotland.

That said, we know the UK regional markets. Sub rates in central London aren't sub rates in Glasgow. CIJC labour adjustments vary by region. Sector mix in the South East skews commercial fit-out and data centres; the Midlands tilts industrial; the North West is heavy on residential and education frameworks. We price to the regional reality of the contractor we're working with, not a national average that wouldn't be competitive anywhere.

"Most lost tenders aren't lost on price. They're lost on the qualifications and prelims that make the price defensible."

What I believe about electrical estimating

  • Mark-up is a number you sign off, not one buried in the BOQ. Every REMC tender separates direct cost, prelims, and mark-up as three line items. You see what you're charging and you defend it.
  • The qualifications page wins as many tenders as the price. A clear schedule of inclusions, exclusions, and design assumptions stops main contractors penalising you for missing scope they should have made clearer.
  • Sub enquiries deserve a deadline, not a please-and-thank-you. If your sub quote comes in at 5pm on the day of submission, you've already lost. We give subs 48 hours before tender deadline and chase early.
  • Pay-on-win pricing aligns incentives properly. Our fees sit inside your tender prelims. Win the job and the work cost you nothing on a net-margin basis. Lose it and we still want you back.
  • Speed of comms is half the service. Estimators who answer queries inside two hours during business hours win pricing standards comparisons even when their numbers are identical to slower competitors. It's a discipline.
