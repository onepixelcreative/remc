Local supply chain — for Glasgow tenders

When we price a tender for a Glasgow-based contractor, our enquiry packs go to wholesalers contractors here actually use — CEF Glasgow (Cathcart Road and Cambuslang branches), Edmundson Electrical, City Electrical Factors, Rexel Cambuslang — alongside specialist sub framework rates from central-belt SELECT and ECA member networks. Labour pricing reflects current CIJC rates with the productivity and travel patterns relevant to operatives working in Scotland.

For a contractor based in Manchester, London or Cardiff, the supply chain and labour adjustments shift accordingly. The principle is the same: we price to the regional reality of the contractor we're working with, not a national average that wouldn't be competitive anywhere.

Why Glasgow contractors choose outsourced estimating

The hiring market for senior electrical estimators in central Scotland is tight. A senior in-house estimator in Glasgow now costs roughly £55–60k base, plus pension and overhead — around £74k fully loaded. For most SMB contractors pricing fewer than twelve tenders a month, that cost is hard to justify until volume is already there. The chicken-and-egg trap is real: you can't grow volume without estimating capacity, and you can't justify estimating capacity without volume.

REMC sits in that gap. A Monthly Service Plan gives you the discipline of a senior estimator for a fraction of the loaded cost of a hire, with no recruitment risk and no single point of failure. You scale capacity up or down month-on-month as your pipeline changes.
